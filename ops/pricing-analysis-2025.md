## PART 2: MARKET PRICING PATTERNS

### What the data tells us:

1. **The market is converging on $49-199 USD/mo for AI receptionists** targeting SMBs. This is roughly $65-270 CAD/mo.

2. **The most aggressive competitors (Rosie, My AI Front Desk, Answering AI) are at $49-99 USD/mo** with generous call limits. Rosie specifically targets HVAC and is the most visible competitor in this exact niche.

3. **Traditional answering services cost 3-10x more** for the same volume — this is the comparison Northern Automation should draw.

4. **Per-minute pricing is dying at the SMB level.** Contractors hate unpredictable bills. Flat-rate or tiered-by-calls is winning.

5. **Free trials are standard** — 7-14 days is most common. 30 days is generous but not unheard of.

6. **Annual discounts of 15-20%** are standard across SaaS.

7. **Setup fees are rare** in this category — good that Northern Automation has none.

## PART 3: UNIT ECONOMICS CHECK

### Current pricing ($397 CAD/mo):
- A typical HVAC contractor: 100-300 calls/mo × 2-4 min = 200-1,200 min/mo
- Vapi cost at $0.05-0.15/min = $10-180/mo per client
- **Gross margin at $397 CAD/mo: 55-97%** depending on call volume
- This is healthy, but the price is 2-4x higher than aggressive competitors

### At $197 CAD/mo (~$145 USD):
- Same Vapi costs: $10-180/mo
- Gross margin: 8-95% (tight at the high-call-volume end)
- At median usage (150 calls × 3 min = 450 min × $0.10 = $45 cost): **77% margin**
- Very workable, and competitively positioned

### At $147 CAD/mo (~$108 USD):
- At median usage ($45 cost): **69% margin**
- At high usage ($120 cost): **18% margin** — getting tight
- Still workable for volume play, needs usage monitoring

### Break-even analysis:
- Fixed infra costs are ~$50-100/mo (Mac mini power, domain, minor SaaS)
- At $197 CAD/mo with $45 median Vapi cost: ~$152/client margin
- 50 clients = $7,600/mo margin
- 100 clients = $15,200/mo margin
- 200 clients = $30,400/mo margin
- **This model works at volume. The question is purely: what price maximizes client acquisition speed?**

## PART 4: THE HVAC CONTRACTOR DECISION FRAMEWORK

An HVAC contractor thinks like this:
- "What's this gonna cost me per month?" → Needs to be a simple, small number
- "What do I get?" → Answer my phones, book my jobs, don't miss calls
- "Is it worth it?" → One booked job from a missed call = $300-2,000+ in revenue
- "Can I cancel?" → Must feel zero-risk
- "Do I have to learn software?" → Must be hands-off

**The $397/mo problem:** A trades person sees $397 and thinks "that's almost $5,000 a year" and "I can hire my wife's niece to answer phones for that." It triggers the mental comparison to a part-time hire, not to a traditional answering service. The number is too high to be an impulse yes.

**The $197/mo sweet spot:** Under $200/mo is a different mental category. It's "a couple service calls covers that." It's less than their truck payment. It feels like a utility bill, not a staffing decision.

### Why This Specific Structure:

#### 1. Single plan, no tiers → Easy yes
Tiers create decision paralysis. An HVAC contractor doesn't want to think about which plan fits. One plan, one price, everything included. Rosie uses tiers and it works for them, but Northern Automation's advantage is simplicity at every step — AI sells, AI onboards, AI runs. Match that with simple pricing.

#### 2. $197/mo instead of $397/mo → 2x+ faster acquisition
- $197 CAD is ~$145 USD — competitive with Rosie's mid-tier ($149 USD) and below Slang, Numa, and others
- Below the $200 psychological threshold
- "Pays for itself with one extra booked call per month" becomes extremely believable at this price
- At scale (100+ clients), total revenue at $197 is far higher than revenue at $397 with 30% fewer signups. **Volume pricing wins when acquisition is automated.**

#### 3. 14-day trial instead of 30 → Faster conversion, lower abuse
- 30 days is too long. Contractors will forget they signed up, or use the full month of free service during busy season and cancel.
- 14 days is enough to prove value (they'll receive real calls within days) but creates urgency.
- Industry standard for this category is 7-14 days. 14 is generous but reasonable.
- You can always extend for a specific prospect — but the default should drive action.

#### 4. $1,997/year → Clean number, real savings
- $367/year savings (15.5% discount) — meaningful but not margin-destroying
- $1,997 is a clean, memorable number
- Positions annual as the "smart business owner" choice
- At $1,997/yr your margin is strong even for high-usage clients

#### 5. No setup fee (keep this) → Removes last objection
- "Nothing to lose, try it free for 14 days, cancel if you don't love it"
- This is the pitch the AI delivers on the cold call. Zero friction.

## PART 6: RISKS & MITIGATIONS

**Risk: High-usage clients erode margin at $197**
→ Mitigation: Monitor Vapi costs per client. If a client consistently exceeds 1,000 min/mo ($100-150 in Vapi costs), that's still a 25%+ margin. Only act if you find clients above 2,000 min/mo, and even then, a gentle usage conversation is better than a tier system.

**Risk: $197 is "too cheap" and signals low quality**
→ Mitigation: Unlikely in this market. HVAC contractors are not SaaS buyers who equate price with quality. They equate price with "is this worth it." The demo video proves quality; the price proves accessibility.

**Risk: 14-day trial isn't long enough**
→ Mitigation: Any HVAC contractor will receive real inbound calls within 48 hours of going live. 14 days is plenty. The risk of a 30-day trial (freeloading, forgetfulness) outweighs the risk of 14 days being too short.

**Risk: Competitors drop to $49/mo and undercut you**
→ Mitigation: At $49 USD they're losing money on any client with real call volume. Those products will either have minute caps, quality issues, or disappear. Your AI-sells-AI model means your acquisition cost is near-zero — that's the real competitive moat, not price.

---

*Bottom line: Drop to $197/mo, shorten the trial to 14 days, keep annual at $1,997/yr, and keep it dead simple — one plan, one price, zero friction. Then let the AI army dial.*
